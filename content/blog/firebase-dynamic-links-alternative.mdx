## What Happened to Firebase Dynamic Links

If you clicked a link in your app recently and landed on a 404 or a dead page, Firebase Dynamic Links is probably the reason.

Google officially shut down Firebase Dynamic Links on **August 25, 2025**. No warning. No migration tool. No legacy redirect support. Just a deprecation notice buried in an email, and then silence.

For the millions of apps that relied on Dynamic Links for onboarding flows, referral campaigns, magic links, and in-app routing, this was a serious problem. And for a lot of developers, it still is.

This guide covers exactly what happened, why it matters in 2026, and which alternatives are worth your time today.

## Why Google Shut It Down

Firebase Dynamic Links launched in 2015 as a free, easy way to create smart links that worked across iOS, Android, and the web. For a decade, it handled:

- Universal Links (iOS) and App Links (Android)
- Deferred deep linking for post-install routing
- Short URLs with custom domains
- Device-based routing (different destination per platform)

The problem? It stopped being profitable. Google's larger Firebase strategy shifted toward monetized products, and Dynamic Links, being completely free with no clear path to revenue, got cut.

The announcement came on October 24, 2023. The shutdown date was August 25, 2025. That's roughly 22 months of notice, which sounds reasonable until you realize most teams have hundreds of dynamic links embedded in:

- Email campaigns and drip sequences
- Push notification templates
- QR codes on physical products and printed materials
- In-app share buttons
- Third-party dashboards and attribution tools

Replacing all of that takes real time and resources, and a lot of teams were caught unprepared.

## What Broke and What You Need to Fix

Every link with a `page.link` domain or a custom Firebase domain that routed through Dynamic Links stopped working on the shutdown date.

Here's what you probably still need to fix if you're reading this in 2026:

**In your codebase:** Any hardcoded Firebase dynamic link URLs. Search your repo for `page.link` and replace them.

**In your email templates:** Magic links, onboarding sequences, and transactional emails that used dynamic links for app routing. Update these in your ESP dashboard.

**In your app's share function:** If your app generates share links programmatically, you need to update the SDK call to use a new provider.

**In print materials and QR codes:** These are the hardest to fix because physical copies can't be updated remotely. If you printed QR codes that point to Firebase links, you need to reprint or use a redirect layer.

### 6. Self-Hosted Redirect Server: Best for Full Control

**Cost:** Infrastructure only | **Account required:** No | **Maintenance:** High

The concept is simple: a server reads the User-Agent header from an incoming request and redirects accordingly. iOS devices go to your custom URI scheme or Universal Link, Android devices go to your App Link or Play Store URL, everything else goes to the web.

A basic Node.js implementation looks like this:

```js
app.get('/link/:id', (req, res) => {
  const ua = req.headers['user-agent'] || '';
  if (/iPhone|iPad/.test(ua)) return res.redirect(iosDeepLink);
  if (/Android/.test(ua)) return res.redirect(androidDeepLink);
  res.redirect(webFallback);
});
```

**Best for:** Engineering teams with DevOps experience and a strong preference for no external dependencies.

**Limitations:** No deferred deep linking without additional session fingerprinting logic. No analytics out of the box. Significant edge case complexity (Facebook in-app browser, WeChat, Instagram browser, etc.).

## How to Migrate Your Firebase Dynamic Links

### Step 1: Find All Your Links

Search your codebase for `page.link` and any custom Firebase domains you configured. Also check email templates, push notification content, and marketing copy.

### Step 2: Pick Your Tool Based on What You Actually Need

Ask these three questions:

1. Do you need to track which marketing campaign drove which app install? If yes, you need Branch, AppsFlyer, Adjust, or Kochava.
2. Do you need to route new users to a specific in-app screen after install? If yes, you need deferred deep linking. Branch has the best free tier for this.
3. Do you just need links that open the right app on the right device? If yes, [DeepLinkOS](/) is the fastest free option and requires no setup.

### Step 3: Create Replacement Links

For each Firebase link, create an equivalent in your new tool. With DeepLinkOS, this is literally:

1. Paste the destination URL
2. The platform gets detected automatically
3. Click to generate the smart link
4. Copy the new URL

That's the whole process. You can read our full guide on [how to create a deep link](/blog/how-to-create-a-deep-link) for step-by-step instructions.

### Step 4: Update Every Surface

Work through your checklist:

- Email templates in your ESP
- Push notification content
- In-app share sheet logic (requires a code update)
- Social media bios and pinned posts
- QR codes (regenerate or use a redirect layer)
- Any paid campaigns still running with old URLs

### Step 5: Test on Real Devices

Always test on actual iOS and Android hardware, not simulators. Check:

- Does the link open the right app?
- Does the fallback web page load when the app is not installed?
- Do in-app browsers (Instagram, TikTok, Facebook) handle the redirect correctly?
