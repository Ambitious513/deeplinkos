## What Is a Deep Link

Think about the last time you tapped a link in an email and landed on the home screen of an app instead of the product you were actually looking at. That is a broken deep link experience.

A deep link is a URL that takes you to a specific location inside a mobile app, not just the app itself. Instead of opening Instagram to your feed, a deep link opens Instagram to a specific post or profile. Instead of opening Spotify to its home screen, it opens a particular playlist.

The "deep" part refers to going deeper into the app's navigation than just the front door.

For users, this feels natural and seamless. For developers and marketers, it is one of the most effective ways to reduce friction and improve conversion rates in mobile apps.

## How Deep Links Actually Work

When you tap a deep link, here is what happens under the hood:

1. Your device's operating system reads the URL
2. If the URL matches a registered app (via Universal Links on iOS or App Links on Android), the OS opens the app directly
3. The app receives the URL and navigates to the appropriate screen
4. If no matching app is found, the OS falls back to a web browser or redirects to the app store

The key phrase in step 2 is "registered app." For deep links to work reliably on iOS and Android, the app developer needs to set up the association between their domain and their app. On iOS, this involves an Apple App Site Association (AASA) file. On Android, it involves a Digital Asset Links (DAL) file.

Tools like [DeepLinkOS](/) handle the routing logic on the redirect side, but the app itself still needs to be configured to handle incoming links.

## The Three Types of Deep Links

### Standard Deep Links

These are the most basic form. They open a specific screen in an app if and only if the app is already installed on the device. If the app is not installed, the link fails or redirects to a generic page.

Standard deep links typically use a custom URI scheme like `youtube://` or `instagram://`, followed by a path to the specific content.

**Best for:** Sending existing users to specific in-app screens. Not suitable for new user acquisition.

### Deferred Deep Links

Deferred deep links solve the "app not installed" problem. When someone taps a deferred deep link and does not have the app installed, the system stores the intended destination. After the user installs the app and opens it for the first time, the app automatically navigates to the original destination.

This is particularly powerful for:

- Referral programs (user taps an invite link, installs the app, lands on the referral reward screen)
- Paid campaigns (user clicks an ad, installs the app, lands on the promoted product)
- Email marketing (user taps a link, installs, arrives at the right content)

Deferred deep linking requires a third-party SDK like Branch, AppsFlyer, or Adjust. It cannot be implemented with simple redirect logic alone.

### Contextual Deep Links

Contextual deep links are deferred deep links with extra data attached. They carry information like referral codes, affiliate IDs, discount codes, or personalization data through the install process.

For example, a referral link might carry the referrer's user ID so both the new user and the referrer can be credited correctly, even if the new user installs the app days later.

## Deep Links vs Regular Links vs Smart Links

People use these terms interchangeably, which causes a lot of confusion. Here is the actual difference:

**Regular link:** Opens a web page in a browser. Always. That is all it does.

**Deep link:** Opens a specific screen inside an installed mobile app. If the app is not installed, it may fail or fall back to a browser.

**Smart link (or universal link in the marketing sense):** A link that detects the user's device and context, then routes them to the best destination. On iOS with the app installed, it opens the app. On Android, it opens the Android app. On desktop, it opens the web. This is what [DeepLinkOS](/) generates.

Note: Apple uses "Universal Links" to describe their specific iOS deep linking technology, which is a different meaning than the marketing term "smart link."

## Why Deep Links Matter for Mobile Growth

The data on deep links is compelling. Mobile apps that use deep links consistently see:

**Higher conversion rates.** Landing users on the exact screen they expected means fewer drop-offs. Someone who taps a product link and lands on that product in-app converts better than someone who lands on the home screen and has to search for it again.

**Better retention from campaigns.** Push notifications with deep links see significantly higher click-through rates because users know they will land somewhere relevant.

**Smoother referral programs.** Deferred deep linking makes referral flows work properly across the install gap, meaning more successful referrals get tracked and credited.

**Improved email engagement.** Email campaigns that deep link into apps (rather than forcing browser sessions) get higher engagement and lower unsubscribe rates for mobile-first audiences.

## Common Use Cases

Here are the situations where teams reach for deep links most often:

**Social sharing:** A user shares a song, video, or product. The link opens the right screen in the app for anyone who has it installed, and falls back to the web for everyone else.

**Email campaigns:** A transactional email includes a link to view an order in-app, access a receipt, or complete a pending action.

**Push notifications:** A notification about a sale or new content links directly to the relevant in-app screen.

**QR codes:** A QR code on packaging or in a store opens a product page in an app rather than a website.

**Paid advertising:** An ad on Instagram or TikTok drives users to a specific app screen, with deferred deep linking handling the post-install routing.

**Magic links for auth:** An authentication email sends a one-tap login link that opens the app directly to the logged-in state.
